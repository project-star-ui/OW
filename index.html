The Super Small Intestine!

Your food's amazing journey through the digestive system

Meet Your Small Intestine!

The small intestine is the longest part of your digestive system - almost 20 feet long! But don't worry, it's neatly coiled up inside your belly.

How Does It Work?

1. Food Arrival

After your stomach breaks food into smaller pieces, it sends this mixture (called chyme) to your small intestine.

2. Nutrient Extraction

Tiny finger-like projections called villi absorb nutrients like vitamins and proteins into your bloodstream.

3. Job Done!

After about 4-6 hours, the leftover parts move to the large intestine where water gets absorbed.

Amazing Small Intestine Facts

Super Long Tube

If stretched out, your small intestine would be about as long as a school bus! But thanks to its coils, it fits nicely in your belly.

Surface Area

All the villi give your small intestine the surface area of a tennis court! This helps absorb nutrients quickly.

Speedy Movers

Food moves through your small intestine at about 1 cm per minute. That's like watching a snail race!

Keeping Your Small Intestine Happy

Eat Good Foods

Fruits, vegetables, and whole grains help your small intestine work its best. Try to eat a rainbow of colors!

Drink Water

Water helps move food through your digestive system. Aim for 6-8 glasses a day!

Stay Active

Exercise helps everything in your body work better - including your small intestine!
